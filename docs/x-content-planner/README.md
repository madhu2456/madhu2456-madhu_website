# X Content Planner

Purpose: use this folder as the working system for promoting Madhu Dadi's portfolio and technical blog on X.

## Strategy

- Primary goal: role inbound from AI, product, analytics, and engineering leaders.
- Cadence: 5 posts per week, Monday through Friday.
- Voice: practical builder. Direct, technical, concrete, and low-hype.
- Core CTA: make the portfolio and blog easy to inspect without sounding like an ad.

## Content Pillars

1. Proof of work
   - Adticks: parallel SEO/AEO/GEO crawler that compares server HTML with rendered DOM.
   - Python and AI Learning Platform: technical blog with search, structured series, and source-grounded Ask AI.
   - Udemy Enroller: FastAPI, Playwright, Celery, Redis, PostgreSQL, Docker, and telemetry.

2. Builder notes
   - Tradeoffs from shipping RAG, agent, automation, and full-stack systems.
   - Production details: evals, guardrails, logging, p95 latency, handoff docs, and failure modes.

3. Blog amplification
   - Turn technical articles into compact lessons.
   - Turn learning series into multi-post arcs.
   - Turn the Ask AI assistant into examples of grounded RAG and retrieval UX.

4. Hiring signal
   - Show 9+ years across AI, analytics, product engineering, and marketing measurement.
   - Make the stack legible: Python, TypeScript, FastAPI, Next.js, SQL, BigQuery, PostgreSQL, RAG, agents, Playwright.
   - Keep the signal anchored in real systems, not generic availability posts.

## Weekly Rhythm

- Monday: portfolio or project proof point.
- Tuesday: practical technical lesson from the blog.
- Wednesday: AI, RAG, or agent engineering opinion with concrete detail.
- Thursday: analytics or measurement insight.
- Friday: soft CTA to portfolio, case study, blog post, or contact.

## Drafting Rules

- Keep most posts below 260 visible characters before the link when possible.
- Use one specific detail per post: metric, stack, tradeoff, failure mode, or system behavior.
- Prefer "I built", "I learned", "I use", and "I care about" over abstract claims.
- Avoid engagement bait, vague AI takes, excessive hashtags, and unsupported performance claims.
- Use at most one hashtag only when it genuinely helps discovery.
